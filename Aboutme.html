About Me

Hello! My name is Tom Studley. I'm originally from Sydney's Inner West in Australia, now studying psychology at the Australian National University in Canberra. One of my favourite past-times is getting lost in the worlds of fantasy and sci-fi novels, movies and games. I have a deep appreciation for the immersive capability of computer graphics. It is my dream to be able to render fantastic, new worlds that others can experience.

My Passion

Blender is a 3D creative software that allows modelling, rigging, animation, simulation, rendering, compositing, motion tracking, and much more. Blender is the go-to for beginners in 3D graphics. It serves as an all-purpose, artistic and professional design medium. It is also completely free, open source, and has a more flexible and beginner-friendly interface than comparable software.

My website is designed specifically as a platform for learners and general users of Blender. Specifically, it is for those who want to discovering interesting elements and techniques that they can apply in their own projects, and perhaps also learn skills while doing so. Often learners are forced to scour through algorithms on YouTube or forums to find tutorials that explain each step thoroughly enough for their understanding. Furthermore, many of the well-known beginner-friendly tutorials are simply boring, as the end goal is to learn, rather than create something cool. I personally do not resonate with this approach, and I think that people are more likely to actively learn when they are fulfilling a goal that they find creatively stimulating.

This website contains a compilation ofhand-picked beginner-friendly tutorials where the objective is to encourage creativity. For example, you could spend 10 hours following an ad-filled, learner-focused tutorial course on creating a regular glazed donut, or you could spend a few hours creating this awesome wireframe donut, which you can immediately edit and play around with in your own scenes.

Each postincludes my own description of the element, and an embedded link to the tutorial.
