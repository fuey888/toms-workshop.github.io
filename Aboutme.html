About Me

Hello! My name is Tom Studley. I grew up in Sydney's Inner West in Australia, and am now studying psychology at the Australian National University. One of my favourite past-times is getting lost in the worlds of fantasy and sci-fi novels, movies and games. I have gained a deep appreciation for the immersive capability of computer graphics. It is my dream to be able to render fantastic, new worlds that others can experience.

My Passion

On the Gallery page, you can view my personal projects created using the program Blender (some are labelled placeholders).

Blender is a 3D creative software that allows modelling, rigging, animation, simulation, rendering, compositing, motion tracking, and much more. Blender is the go-to for beginners in 3D graphics. It serves as an all-purpose, artistic and professional design medium. It is also completely free, open source, and has a more flexible and beginner-friendly interface than comparable software like Maya.

The Guidebook contains a compilation of hand-picked beginner-friendly Blender tutorials where the objective is to inspire creativity. I believe that the best way to learn is by doing something you enjoy. You could bore yourself following a 10 hour, ad-filled tutorial course to create a regular glazed donut, or you could spend a few hours creating this awesome wireframe donut. I hope that users will use these videos to draw inspiration for their own personal projects.


Thanks for reading and be sure to follow and share my work through the social media via the social media buttons in the Index! Please get in touch on the Contact form if you have any questions, requests, or constructive feedback.
